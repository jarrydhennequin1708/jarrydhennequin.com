Australian Financial Review Web Development Data Visualization

AFR's Most Innovative Companies Microsite

Designed and developed an interactive microsite for AFR's Most Innovative Companies awards in just 3 weeks, enabling users to explore company data, trends, and sector insights through engaging visualizations and responsive design.

Challenge

The Australian Financial Review wanted to create an interactive digital experience for their annual Most Innovative Companies awards. With a tight three-week timeline and complex data requirements, the project demanded rapid execution without sacrificing quality:

  1. Extremely tight timeline of just 3 weeks from concept to launch.
  2. Complex company data needed to be presented in an engaging, accessible way.
  3. Required responsive design that worked flawlessly across all devices.
  4. Interactive data visualizations needed to be both informative and visually appealing.
  5. Integration with AFR's existing brand guidelines while creating a unique awards experience.

Approach

To deliver this project within the compressed timeline, I implemented a streamlined design and development process:

  1. Rapid Prototyping: Created high-fidelity prototypes early to align stakeholder expectations and reduce iteration cycles.
  2. Component-Based Design: Built a modular design system that accelerated development and ensured consistency.
  3. Interactive Data Viz: Designed intuitive data visualizations that make complex information accessible to all users.
  4. Mobile-First Approach: Ensured the experience worked seamlessly on mobile devices where many users would access the content.
  5. Performance Optimization: Optimized assets and code to ensure fast load times despite rich visual content.

Outcomes and Impact

Despite the aggressive timeline, the project was delivered successfully and achieved excellent results:

  1. On-Time Delivery: Launched the fully functional microsite within the 3-week deadline.
  2. Strong Engagement: Users spent significant time exploring company data and sector trends through the interactive visualizations.
  3. Cross-Device Success: The responsive design performed excellently across desktop, tablet, and mobile devices.
  4. Positive Stakeholder Feedback: AFR and award sponsors expressed strong satisfaction with the final product.
  5. Template for Future Events: The modular design system created a foundation for future awards microsites.

This project demonstrates the ability to deliver high-quality digital experiences under tight deadlines without compromising on design quality or user experience. The success of the microsite helped establish a new standard for AFR's digital awards coverage.
